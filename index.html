Research vision

Building trustworthy wireless infrastructure for a connected future.

Next-generation wireless networks will increasingly connect industrial automation, critical infrastructure, autonomous systems, and large-scale IoT. These systems must operate efficiently in crowded spectrum environments while remaining secure against both known and evolving threats.

My long-term academic goal is to lead collaborative, student-engaged research on secure and resilient wireless systems. I aim to bridge theory, simulation, and experimentation to develop practical mechanisms for fair spectrum sharing, dependable access, privacy-preserving connectivity, and secure deployment of future 6G technologies.

Research agenda

Secure and resilient communication for 5G, 6G, and beyond.

My work lies at the intersection of cybersecurity, wireless communication, and IoT. It examines how future networks can share spectrum fairly, detect malicious behavior, and deliver reliable services in environments where failures or attacks can have real operational consequences.

01

Coexistence security and spectrum access

Characterizing vulnerabilities in unlicensed and shared spectrum, including 5G NR-U and Wi-Fi coexistence, and designing mechanisms for fair, dependable channel access.

02

Anomaly-aware resilience

Developing detection, mitigation, and adaptive access approaches that preserve throughput, reduce harmful interference, and improve resilience under adversarial behavior.

03

Private 5G/6G and critical applications

Securing wireless connectivity for industrial automation, critical infrastructure, and IoT deployments where reliability, latency, and safety matter.

04

Emerging 6G security directions

Extending this agenda toward secure mmWave/THz systems, UAV/UAS communications, RIS-assisted links, and physical-layer security for future autonomous networks.

Research approach and platforms: I combine analytical and stochastic models with network simulation and wireless testbed experimentation. My workflow includes Python, ns-3, SimPy, MATLAB, OMNeT++, OpenAirInterface, software-defined radio platforms, Linux, Wireshark, Git/GitHub, SQL, Java, C++, C#, and Swift.

Representative contributions

From vulnerabilities to adaptive resilience.

Across my recent work, I investigate how timing, channel access, mobility, and anomalous energy behavior can be exploited in shared-spectrum networks—and how those systems can be strengthened without sacrificing fairness.

2026

Ghost Rider

An anomaly-aware hybrid reservation and self-deferral framework that strengthens 5G/B5G operation in the 5 GHz unlicensed band.

2025

Z-Force

A Z-score-powered anomaly-detection framework for promoting fair 5G NR-U and Wi-Fi coexistence in the 5 GHz band.

2024–2025

CAD and RanCAD

Attack models that expose channel-access deterrence vulnerabilities affecting NR-U and Wi-Fi coexistence in unlicensed spectrum.

2023

PACMAN

A mobility-powered attack model for private 5G-enabled industrial automation, highlighting security risks in critical wireless deployments.
